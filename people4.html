Salah Harb

Postdoctoral Researcher

Salah Harb was born in Irbid, Jordan, in 1988. He received his B.S. and M.S. degrees in Computer Engineering from Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST), Jordan, in 2011 and 2014, respectively. He earned his Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Concordia University, Montreal, Canada, in 2023, where his research focused on the hardware implementation of cryptosystems using reconfigurable platforms, as well as signal and image processing systems. Following his doctoral studies, Dr. Harb worked as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL), California, where his research centered on distributed fiber-optic sensing systems and high-performance signal processing architectures. He is currently a Research Fellow at Florida Institute of Technology (FIT), where his work focuses on the design and development of cloud-based AI/ML platforms for large-scale medical datasets, integrating data engineering, scalable analytics, and machine learning workflows to support medical research and clinical insight generation. His research expertise spans reconfigurable and embedded system design, cryptographic hardware acceleration, signal and image processing, fiber-optic sensing, and cloud-based AI/ML systems for data-driven applications.
